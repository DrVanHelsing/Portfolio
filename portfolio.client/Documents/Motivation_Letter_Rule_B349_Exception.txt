This role is substantively research in nature. It demands the precise analytical, experimental,

UWC research department speaks directly to the institution's own confidence in my capacity to

** Telkom AI Call Centre Management Solution — TelkomXHackathon, Winner (August 2025) **

Competing in the TelkomXHackathon, my team developed and delivered a production-ready
AI-assisted call centre platform — the winning submission. The system comprised an ASP.NET Core
(.NET 8) API orchestrator, a React-based real-time supervisor dashboard (SignalR), a
cross-platform .NET MAUI client for Android, iOS, Windows, and Mac Catalyst, and integration
with Azure Cognitive Services for transcription, translation, and AI-assisted response
generation. This project required systems architecture reasoning, real-time distributed systems
design, and cloud integration — competencies directly at the intersection of engineering
research and applied innovation.


** FinanceBuddy — SA Intervarsity Hackathon, 3rd Place (AI Category, September 2025) **

At the South African Intervarsity Hackathon, I led the development of FinanceBuddy, an
AI-powered financial wellness application that earned third place in the AI category. The
solution combined .NET MAUI, ASP.NET Core, Azure OpenAI (GPT-4), Azure Translator, and a
gamification engine to produce a multi-platform, multi-language wellness companion. The judging
criteria for this category emphasised innovation, technical rigour, and real-world impact —
and placement at a national intervarsity level reflects a standard of work recognised across
the academic community.


** StudentLink — AI-Powered Career Readiness Platform (April 2025 – Ongoing) **

StudentLink is a multi-platform career solution I designed and am actively building, consisting
of an ASP.NET Core Web API backend, a React frontend, and a .NET MAUI mobile application. It
incorporates an ML-driven job recommendation engine using embeddings and feature engineering,
AI-powered CV analysis and scoring, and end-to-end application workflow management. The
architecture, design documentation, and iterative development methodology I have applied to
this project are consistent with the standards of applied research in software engineering and
human-computer interaction.


** Neural Networks Implementation — CSC312 Research Assignments **

My formal undergraduate machine learning work — structured across three Jupyter Notebook
assignments covering linear regression, logistic regression, and feedforward neural networks
with full backpropagation — demonstrates applied competence in the mathematical foundations
of AI. These assignments explored cost function design, gradient descent optimisation,
regularisation, decision boundary analysis, and hyperparameter tuning, establishing the
rigorous theoretical grounding necessary for postgraduate-level AI research.


** Structural Geology Field Mapping Simulator — Computational Geoscience Research Tool **

A browser-based 3D geological field simulator that procedurally generates a 2 km × 2 km
terrain using multi-octave noise functions and hydraulic erosion, models six stratigraphic
layers deformed by regional folds and a normal fault, and provides a suite of virtual field
instruments — rock identification, inclined borehole drilling with configurable azimuth and
inclination, strike-and-dip orientation measurement, and geological cross-section rendering.
The rendering pipeline implements a physically-based Cook-Torrance BRDF terrain shader, SSAO
post-processing, atmospheric Rayleigh/Mie scattering, and ACES filmic tone mapping — all
running in the browser on commodity hardware.

The research potential of this project spans computational geoscience education, procedural
subsurface modelling, and HCI design for scientific field instruments. It demonstrates the
capacity to translate domain knowledge from the natural sciences into rigorous, research-quality
software artefacts — precisely the kind of cross-disciplinary synthesis that characterises
innovative postgraduate research.


** UWC Postgraduate Portal — Institutional Workflow Research Platform **

A full-stack, role-based postgraduate request management system developed specifically for UWC,
supporting six user roles (Student, Supervisor, Coordinator, Admin, External, Examiner) across
the full Higher Degree Request lifecycle — from draft submission through Faculty and Senate
Board approval. The system features a dynamic form engine with 20 prebuilt HDR templates and
15 field types, an inline annotation and text-highlight system, multi-version document review
with PDF annotation, a digital signature framework, cross-user email notifications, an
admin-facing drag-and-drop form builder, and role-specific guided walkthrough tours.

The research relevance of this project is direct. It models and automates the institutional
processes governing postgraduate research administration at UWC itself, and demonstrates
capability in systems design for academic governance, human-computer interaction in high-stakes
workflow environments, and the digital transformation of university administrative
infrastructure. This project could naturally serve as the foundation for applied research in
e-governance, academic workflow optimisation, or institutional HCI.


** Physics IDE — Computational Physics Pedagogy Platform **

A dual-mode integrated development environment for physics simulation, combining a Blockly-based
visual block editor with a full GlowScript/VPython code editor and a live, iframe-isolated 3D
simulation viewport. The system is designed to scaffold computational physics literacy —
enabling beginners to construct simulations visually while exposing the generated code,
supporting a progressive transition to full text-based programming. Templates cover projectile
motion, orbital mechanics, and gravitational dynamics, with engineering care taken to prevent
numerical instabilities through softened gravitational distances and deterministic runtime
isolation per execution.

The research potential of this project sits at the intersection of computational physics
education, programming language accessibility, and visual programming paradigm design — areas
of active investigation in both computer science and physics education research. The IDE
addresses a concrete pedagogical problem: lowering the barrier to physics simulation without
sacrificing the conceptual depth that code-level reasoning demands.
